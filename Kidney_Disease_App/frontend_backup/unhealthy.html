Health Alert: CKD Risk Detected

Your health parameters suggest a potential risk of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Please consult a healthcare professional immediately.

Confidence Level: 85%

🔴 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS REQUIRED

⚡ Priority 1: Consult a Nephrologist

  • Schedule an appointment with a kidney specialist (Nephrologist) as soon as possible
  • Get comprehensive kidney function tests (Creatinine, GFR, BUN, Urinalysis)
  • Blood pressure monitoring

💛 DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS for CKD

  • Limit Sodium: Reduce to 1500-2300mg per day
  • Control Protein: Moderate intake (doctor-recommended amount)
  • Monitor Potassium: Limit foods high in potassium
  • Restrict Phosphorus: Avoid dairy, nuts, seeds
  • Fluid Intake: May need to limit depending on kidney stage
  • Avoid Processed Foods: They contain high sodium and additives

🏋️ LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS

  • Regular Exercise: Moderate physical activity (consult doctor first)
  • Blood Pressure Control: Keep below 130/80 mmHg
  • Quit Smoking: Smoking accelerates kidney damage
  • Limit Alcohol: No more than 1 drink per day for women, 2 for men
  • Reduce Stress: Practice meditation and yoga
  • Adequate Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours per night

⚕️ MEDICAL MANAGEMENT

  • Take blood pressure medications as prescribed
  • Monitor blood sugar (if diabetic)
  • Avoid NSAIDs and similar pain relievers
  • Regular lab work and monitoring
  • Follow nephrologist's treatment plan

📋 TESTS YOU SHOULD GET DONE

  • Serum Creatinine Test
  • eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate)
  • Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
  • Urinalysis
  • Blood Pressure Check
  • Electrolyte Panel
  • Renal Ultrasound or Imaging

🚨 SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL CARE IF:

  • Sudden decrease in urine output
  • Severe swelling in legs, ankles, or face
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Severe headache with vision changes
  • Blood in urine
